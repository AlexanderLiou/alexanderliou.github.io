2022 FRC Robot, Team 1072

Summary

This FIRST Robotics Competition robot was built to collect and launch balls into a goal, and it can climb an ascending monkey bar-like structure. It competed in the 2022 FIRST Robotics Competition league with the objective of scoring points. This robot performed at the 94th world percentile in competitions. I designed the majority of its subsystems using CAD through a process of iterative testing.

  • 94th percentile performance among global teams. Highest statistical offensive power performance rating in the history of FRC team 1072.
  • 6 week design and build time.

How

  • Objectives, constraints, rules, and strategy informed design choice of a complex machine.
  • Designed by breaking into five modular subsytems that integrate.
  • Drivetrain: omnidirectional swerve drive, translates in any direction.
  • Intake: deployable roller mechanism on four bar linkages picks up balls from the floor.
  • Indexer: stores balls and moves them through the robot.
  • Shooter: Continuously adjustable trajectory achieved through flywheel ball shooter with computer vision targeting.
  • Climber: Linear telescoping arms on pneumatic actuation combined with separate latching hooks facilitated climbing an assembly of ascending monkey bars.

Challenges

  • Difficulty predicting the dynamics of the climb led to rapid iteration of specific hook parts to optimize positioning for faster climbing after determining initial geometry requirements.
  • Shooter mechanism range was limited by mechanical trajectory limits, as doctrine and game strategy shifted toward longer range throughout the season.
  • Uncertainty in game piece performance led to rapid prototyping of intake, shooter, and indexer mechanisms.

Details

Below is the robot climbing an ascending monkey bar truss. It does so via telescoping linear arms with hooks, with a pneumatic actuation of the arms between two fixed positions. Passively latching hooks integrated with the superstructure enable moving from one bar to the next.

The video below depicts the robot practicing gameplay, in which it uses its deployable intake to pick up balls and shoots them into a goal using an adjustable flywheel shooter. A defending robot attempts to block and impede the scoring robot.

Notable design features:

  • Flywheel shooter featured double flywheel design propelling ball from top and bottom flywheels linked to the same drive system.
  • Shooter angle adjusted via rack and pinion mechanism and an independent motor on a high gear reduction.
  • Various retroreflective internal sensors determine ball locations to automatically move ball indexer.
  • Pneumatic cylinders actuate intake and climber arms between two positions.
  • Retractable roller intake mounted on polycarbonate four-bar to reduce damage done by impacts and enhance deployment speed.
  • Iterative rapid prototypes used to evaluate concepts (used modular materials and 3D printing)
