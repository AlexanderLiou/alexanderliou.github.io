3D Printed Projects

This two-axis gimbal stabilizes a mounting for modular devices from rotations of the hand. It uses a raspberry pi to compute compensation from a gyroscope and outputs a pwm signal to servo motors to maintain level on the end of the gimbal.

This soldering iron linear guide uses a double reverse four bar linkage with built-in gear teeth to move a soldering iron vertically for installing heat set inserts into my 3D printed parts. It didn't work particularly well due to tolerance issues with 3D prints and complexity, but it was fun to make.

These bike water bottle cages have a sponsor's logo on them for triathlons.

This two-stage 3D printed elevator extends in a telescoping cascade motion, where each stage slides past the other simultaneously. It lifts by a drum with string rigging and a rack and pinion drive.
