Payload Release Quadcopter

Summary

This custom drone was built from scratch and stabilizes itself in flight using gyroscope inputs to a PID control loop. It is capable of carrying a small payload that it can deliver by dropping. It is controlled by radio transmitter and can lift more than 60 grams with ease while maintaining a thrust to weight ratio of 4.7:1, enabling ample acceleration. I incorporated modified open source code uploaded to hardware mounted on a custom frame that I designed for modularity, ease of manufacturing, and ease of repair. The drone's payload is released via a servo motor-actuated gripper, and any small round object or casing for an object can be carried.

How

  • An Arduino Uno microcontroller acts as a flight controller.
  • Gyroscope (MPU6050) input to a tuned PID loop maintains stability in flight unless commanded otherwise by operator.
  • Using Fusion 360, custom-designed 3D printed frame designed for easy replacement of parts broken in-flight.
  • COTS electronics, motors, and speed controllers.
  • Manual wiring to accommodate battery voltage measurement circuit via voltage division.

Challenges

  • Manual PID tuning of gain values for my specific frame properties.
  • Design of frame to create predictable failure modes with 3D printed parts for easy replacement.
  • Designing to minimize displacement of center of mass from the geometric middle of the frame.
  • Learning curve for electrical systems work.
