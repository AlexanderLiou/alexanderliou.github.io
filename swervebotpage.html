Swerve Drive Robot

Summary

This small scale swerve drive robot was built as a circuit analysis class final project with a partner (Jackie Liu), incorporating interdisciplinary engineering fields. Controlled by an Arduino, the robot uses positional rotation servo motors to direct the wheels in the correct direction and continuous rotation servos to drive the wheels in their respective headings. The controller uses a separate Arduino with a bluetooth module transmitter, sending gyroscope angles of the controller to the robot. The limited 180 degree travel angle of the positional rotation servos led to the requirement of modification of the software to achieve certain velocity vectors by reversing the direction of some wheels and running them backward.

  • Gyroscope Arduino controller with bluetooth communication with Arduino-driven robot.
  • Omnidirectional translational drive.
  • Throttleable via controller tilt angle.
  • 3D printed frame and swerve modules.

How

  • Arduino controlled swervo motors via pwm signal for both wheel direction-positioning motors and wheel-driving continuous motors.
  • 3D printed swerve modules mate continuously rotating wheels to an angular positioning servo motor.
  • Gyroscopic inputs from the controller interpreted by the bluetooth module aboard the robot Arduino, and respective heading and velocity values are generated with a basic interpretation of the Euler angles received.

Challenges

  • Difficulty with power sourcing with our limited resources led to use of improvised power system barely sufficient to drive all motors simultaneously.
  • Limited range of motion of the positioning servo motors led to some heading changes requiring a reversal of two out of four swerve modules.
